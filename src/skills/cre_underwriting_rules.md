## Cap rate benchmarks (typical Q4 2025 / 2026 ranges)

- **Multifamily Class A core market:** 4.50% – 5.25%
- **Multifamily Class B value-add:** 5.50% – 6.50%
- **Industrial / logistics last-mile:** 5.00% – 6.00%
- **Industrial bulk warehouse:** 5.50% – 6.75%
- **Retail single-tenant NNN investment-grade:** 5.50% – 6.50%
- **Retail multi-tenant strip / unanchored:** 7.00% – 8.50%
- **Retail anchored center w/ grocery:** 6.00% – 7.50%
- **Office Class A CBD:** 6.50% – 8.00% (very market-dependent post-COVID)
- **Office suburban Class B:** 8.00% – 10.00%

When you cite these, mark them as benchmarks and tell the user the actual market may differ.

## NOI normalization checklist

When the OM's stated NOI looks rosy, walk through:

1. **Vacancy/credit loss:** OMs often use 5%; market actual frequently 7-10%.
2. **Management fee:** add 3-4% of EGI if absent (many OMs assume self-management).
3. **Replacement reserves:** $250-400/unit/yr multifamily, $0.10-0.30/sf retail/industrial, $0.30-0.50/sf office.
4. **Real estate taxes:** check post-sale reassessment risk (CA Prop 13, FL save-our-homes, etc).
5. **Insurance:** in coastal / wildfire / tornado markets, OM number is often 18-30 months out of date.

## Debt sizing math shortcuts

- **DSCR-constrained loan** = NOI / (DSCR × annual debt constant)
- **LTV-constrained loan** = Purchase price × max LTV
- The smaller of the two governs.
- For a 30-yr amort at 7.0%, debt constant ≈ 7.98%. At 6.5% ≈ 7.59%. At 6.0% ≈ 7.20%.

## Quick yield-on-cost test

YoC = stabilized NOI ÷ all-in basis (purchase + cap-ex + closing + lease-up costs).
Target spread to in-place cap rate: +100bps minimum, +150bps better, +200bps strong value-add.

## What a "good" deal looks like by score band

- **Strong Buy (85+):** going-in cap above market, DSCR >1.40, in-place rents below market, no major capex due
- **Buy (70-84):** going-in cap at market, DSCR 1.25-1.40, modest growth path
- **Neutral (50-69):** priced fully, requires execution (lease-up, value-add) to hit returns
- **Pass (30-49):** below-market cap on full-priced rents, no upside path
- **Reject (<30):** negative leverage, structural occupancy issues, or basis well above replacement cost
