## LOI structure (standard 1-2 page format)

1. **Property description** — name, address, parcel ID, approx SF or units
2. **Purchase price** — total, $/SF or $/unit, plus assumed cap rate at closing NOI
3. **Earnest money** — initial deposit (1-2% common), additional after due diligence
4. **Inspection / due diligence period** — 30-60 days standard; complex deals 75-90
5. **Financing contingency** — yes/no; if yes, terms (LTV, rate cap, DSCR floor)
6. **Closing date** — typically 30-45 days after DD waiver
7. **Closing costs** — who pays what (transfer tax, title insurance, escrow, broker fees)
8. **Survey & title** — buyer obtains updated survey, seller delivers clean title
9. **Tenant estoppels & SNDAs** — required from major tenants pre-close
10. **Operating covenants** — seller maintains property in current condition through close
11. **Confidentiality + exclusivity** — 30-60 day no-shop standard
12. **Non-binding** — clear statement that LOI is non-binding except confidentiality + exclusivity

## Negotiation defaults to suggest

- **Earnest money:** start at 1% initial, 2% after DD waiver. Sellers push for 3-5%.
- **DD period:** 60 days for buildings with rent rolls / leases. 30 days for vacant land.
- **Financing contingency:** drop it if bidding competitively; keep it if rate environment is volatile.
- **Closing date:** 30 days post-DD is typical for cash; 45-60 days for financed.
- **Outs:** preserve the right to terminate during DD for any reason.

## When to walk away in negotiation

- Seller refuses standard estoppels from anchor tenants
- Seller wants binding LOI (red flag — usually means broker is shopping)
- Asking price more than 10% above your max with no movement on cap rate
- Seller resists providing rent roll, T-12, and operating expense detail upfront

## Quick price-to-LOI conversion

LOI price = (target stabilized NOI / target going-in cap) − closing costs reserve

Example: $750k NOI / 7.0% cap = $10.71M property value − $50k closing reserve = ~$10.66M LOI bid
