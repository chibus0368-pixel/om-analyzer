# First-Pass Underwriting Brief: New Berlin Strip Center

*This is a first-pass underwriting screen based on the rent roll, Colliers listing flyer, and clearly labeled assumptions. It should be treated as a directional assessment, not a final investment model.*

---

## Deal Snapshot

- Multi-tenant NNN retail strip center — 15459 W Howard Ave, New Berlin, WI 53151
- 14,469 SF gross leasable area across 5 units (4 occupied, 1 vacant)
- Year built 1996, 81 parking spaces, shadow-anchored by Pick N' Save
- **91.7% occupied** — CloudX (1,200 SF / Tenant 3) has vacated mid-lease; space marketed by Colliers at $20/SF NNN
- Two additional near-term lease expirations: Lanai Tanning Salon (2,000 SF, Gross, expired 1/31/2026) and Trenz By Sheri (1,200 SF, NNN, expires 6/30/2026)
- In-place NOI of ~$153K on $240K occupied rent (with CloudX vacancy reflected)
- No asking price provided — analysis uses cap rate scenario range
- Anchor tenant Concentra Health Services, Inc. holds 46.5% of GLA through 2/2033

---

## Initial Read

This is a small suburban Wisconsin strip center that looked like a $177K NOI deal on the original rent roll — but the picture has gotten worse. CloudX, which was Tenant 3 at $9.02/SF on a lease through 1/2028, has already vacated. That's a third problem tenant on top of the two near-term expirations originally flagged. The building now has 1,200 SF sitting empty and being marketed by Colliers at $20/SF NNN, with two more tenants (3,200 SF combined, ~22% of GLA) at risk of rolling in the next few months.

The good news: Concentra Health Services and Rocky Rococo give you ~$180K in annual rent and lock down 70% of GLA through 2029–2033. Those are real tenants in a decent suburban location with $100K+ median household income and 22,500 VPD on S Moorland Rd. The bad news: if Lanai Tanning Salon and Trenz By Sheri also walk, you're down to those two tenants, DSCR goes well below 1.0x, and you're re-leasing 4,400 SF in a market where the Colliers asking rent ($20/SF) is above what most of these tenants were paying. Note that Lanai's lease is Gross (no CAM recovery) while Trenz is NNN. The deal needs a meaningfully lower basis or confirmed renewals to work.

- **Trenz By Sheri expires 6/30/2026** — 1,200 SF, $23,880/yr NNN, no extension option. Renewal status unknown.
- **Total at-risk exposure: 4,400 SF = 30.4% of GLA, ~$77K in rent** — a devastating hit if all three go dark
- **DSCR already marginal at ~1.23x** — drops below 1.0x if Lanai Tanning Salon also vacates
- **No management fee or reserves in owner OpEx** — real NOI is lower than stated
- **Colliers asking $20/SF NNN for vacant space** — CloudX was only paying $9.02/SF. Can the space actually re-lease at $20?
- **Lanai Tanning Salon is Gross lease** — no CAM recovery on 2,000 SF; NNN conversion would improve NOI quality

---

## Wisconsin Context

With the updated vacancy picture, this deal is weaker than the original first pass. A ~$132/SF basis at 8% cap on current income would be on the lower end for suburban Wisconsin strip retail, but the risk profile now demands it. The Colliers asking rent of $20/SF NNN is on the higher end of typical suburban strip rents ($12–$18/SF range), which raises the question of whether they can actually backfill at that rate — especially since CloudX was only paying $9.02. The demographics are solid ($100K+ median income, 140K population in 5 miles, 22,500 VPD), and the Pick N' Save anchor helps drive traffic, but a buyer return hurdle of 7–8% cash-on-cash is typical here and this deal is nowhere close at ~3.5% levered on current income. The deal needs re-pricing or confirmed leasing activity to be competitive in a Wisconsin context.

## First-Pass Conclusion

### Overall Signal
🔴 Weak first look — deteriorating from original assessment

### Recommendation
**Rework assumptions before advancing** — deal has gotten worse since initial screen. Need confirmed leasing activity or a meaningfully lower basis.

### Why
- What started as a 2-tenant rollover risk is now a 3-tenant problem: CloudX gone, Lanai Tanning Salon expired, Trenz By Sheri expiring within months
- 30% of GLA and ~$77K in rent are at risk — that's the deal
- DSCR is already below target at ~1.23x on in-place; adjusted DSCR at ~1.08x is paper-thin
- Cash-on-cash of ~3.5% doesn't clear a typical 7–8% hurdle for Wisconsin suburban strip
- If Lanai Tanning Salon also walks, DSCR goes below 1.0x and the deal breaks
- **Worth a deeper look ONLY IF**: (a) the basis comes down to 9.0–9.5% cap on current income (~$1.6–$1.7M), giving enough cushion to absorb leasing risk; or (b) the seller can show executed renewals/LOIs on Lanai and Trenz By Sheri; or (c) the CloudX space gets leased at or near the $20/SF asking rent
- The core of the deal (Concentra Health Services + Rocky Rococo at $180K/yr on 70% of GLA) is solid — it's the edges that are falling apart
