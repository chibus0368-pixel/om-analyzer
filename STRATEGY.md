# OM Analyzer — Product Strategy & Philosophy

## Core Positioning

OM Analyzer is a **first-pass deal intelligence tool** for commercial real estate.

It is not a full underwriting replacement.

It is designed to help users:
- move faster on deal evaluation
- filter opportunities quickly
- form an initial opinion before deeper analysis

---

## Primary User Types

### 1. CRE Brokers
- Want to present deals with insight
- Need tools to communicate value to clients
- Often share outputs externally

### 2. Investors / Buyers
- Review high volumes of deals
- Need fast signal on "worth pursuing or not"
- Value speed over perfection

### 3. Owner-Operators
- Evaluate repositioning opportunities
- Think in terms of NOI growth and tenant mix
- Care about real-world execution, not just numbers

---

## Core Product Philosophy

### 1. Speed over completeness
- The product should deliver insight quickly
- Missing data is acceptable
- Avoid blocking flows due to incomplete information

---

### 2. Opinion over neutrality
- Outputs should be directional and useful
- Avoid generic or overly cautious language
- Users want help making decisions

---

### 3. Simplicity first
- UI should not feel complex or overwhelming
- Avoid adding features that clutter the primary flow
- Advanced capabilities should be secondary or hidden

---

### 4. Trust through transparency
- Clearly communicate when data is estimated or incomplete
- Avoid presenting uncertain outputs as facts
- Provide confidence signals where appropriate

---

### 5. Assist, don't replace
- The tool supports human decision-making
- It should not attempt to fully automate underwriting
- Users remain the final decision-makers

---

## Core Workflow

### Step 1: Upload
User uploads an OM (PDF, image, or document)

---

### Step 2: Extraction
System extracts structured data:
- property basics
- financials
- rent roll (if available)

---

### Step 3: Analysis
System generates:
- summary of the deal
- key signals
- directional insight

---

### Step 4: Output
User sees:
- high-level summary
- key metrics
- ability to explore deeper

---

### Step 5: Optional Depth
User can:
- adjust assumptions
- explore additional insights
- organize deals into workspace

---

## Feature Design Principles

### Keep primary flow clean
- Upload → Output should be frictionless
- No unnecessary steps

---

### Layer advanced features
- Advanced tools should not be visible by default
- Introduce them only after engagement

Examples:
- "Ask the Deal" panel
- deeper analysis tools
- exports and reports

---

### Avoid feature overload
- Each feature must justify its existence
- Remove or hide anything that adds confusion

---

## "Ask the Deal" Philosophy

This feature is:
- Optional
- Secondary
- Designed for more advanced users

It should:
- not interrupt the core flow
- not appear on the main landing experience
- live inside the property detail experience

Purpose:
- help users think through decisions
- provide directional ideas, not definitive answers

---

## Output Style Guidelines

All outputs should be:
- concise
- structured
- actionable

Avoid:
- long paragraphs
- vague language
- generic advice

Prefer:
- bullet points
- clear recommendations
- practical ideas

---

## Tone
- professional but not corporate
- confident but not absolute
- helpful, not verbose

---

## Non-Goals

OM Analyzer is NOT:
- a full financial model replacement
- a legal or investment advisor
- a guaranteed prediction tool

---

## Future Direction (High Level)

The product will evolve toward:
- deeper deal insight
- better decision support
- more contextual intelligence

But must always:
- remain fast
- remain simple
- remain opinionated

---

## Summary

OM Analyzer exists to answer one question:

**"Is this deal worth my time?"**

Everything in the product should support that goal.
